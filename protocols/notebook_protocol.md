
# Notebook Protocol

---

## Purpose

This protocol defines how executable explorations are constructed within the Field Lab.

Each notebook is not a standalone experiment, but a structured extension of a formal object.

---

## Structural Position

Every notebook must explicitly state:

- The framework it belongs to (e.g., UFO, TIE)
- The structural block it activates (if applicable)

A notebook that cannot be positioned structurally does not belong in the lab.

---

## Required Structure

Each notebook must follow this structure:

### 1. Title

A precise and minimal name describing the structural focus.

---

### 2. Structural Context

What object or regime is being explored?

Example:
UFO — Block 1 (Structural Tension)

---

### 3. Objective

What structural question is being explored?

Not “what are we doing”
But:
What must become visible?

---

### 4. Formal Definition

Relevant definitions, expressions, or objects.

No pedagogy.
Only what is structurally necessary.

---

### 5. Computational Implementation

Executable code.

- Clear
- Minimal
- Reproducible

No decorative code.

---

### 6. Observations

What appears?

Patterns, behaviors, invariances, breakdowns.

No interpretation yet.

---

### 7. Structural Interpretation

What does this reveal about the regime?

This must connect back to the framework.

---

## Constraints

- No notebook depends on another notebook to be understood
- No object is introduced without execution
- No execution without structural relevance
- No explanation that is not required by the structure

---

## Philosophy

The notebook is not a demonstration.

It is a site of interaction.

Structure is not explained.

It is made visible.
