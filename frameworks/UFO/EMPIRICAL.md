# UFO — Empirical Signatures of the Regime

---

## Structural Observation

The structure is not random.

Certain configurations resist factorization.

The question is not whether this happens.

The question is why.

---

## Observed Regularities

### 1. Rank distribution is non-uniform

Higher-rank configurations dominate the sampled space.

Rank-1 configurations appear as a limiting case.

---

### 2. Internal structure within irreducible configurations

Within configurations satisfying rank(Φ) ≥ 2, internal patterns are not uniform.

Configurations sharing irreducibility exhibit distinct internal structures.

---

### 3. Pattern frequency asymmetry

Certain internal zero-minor patterns occur significantly more frequently than others.

This asymmetry persists across samples.

---

### 4. Stability under perturbation

Internal patterns are not equally stable.

Some configurations preserve their structure under small perturbations.

Others collapse.

---

### 5. Magnitude variability

Nonzero minors exhibit non-uniform magnitude distributions.

Internal structure is not only combinatorial, but also weighted.

---

## Interpretation Boundary

No classification of these differences is assumed.

No explanatory model is imposed.

The observations indicate that irreducibility does not determine a unique internal structure.

Further structural descriptors may be required.
